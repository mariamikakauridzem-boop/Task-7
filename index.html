Sky Giants: Iconic Aircraft Through the Years

Each aircraft in this collection tells a story of technological achievement and purpose - whether conquering speed, lifting massive cargo, or dominating the skies in combat - highlighting the milestones that have defined modern aviation.

The Queen of the Skies

Boeing 747

First flown in 1969, primarily a commercial passenger airliner, also used for cargo and VIP transport.

Stealth Supremacy Fighter

Lockheed Martin F-22 Raptor

Introduced in 2005, a stealth air superiority fighter for military combat.

The Pilot's First Choice

Cessna 172 Skyhawk

First flown in 1955, mainly a light general aviation aircraft for training and private use.

Supersonic Luxury Jet

Concorde

Introduced in 1976, a supersonic passenger airliner capable of very high-speed transatlantic flights.

Legendary Military Workhorse

Bell UH-1 Iroquois (“Huey”)

Entered service in 1959, a utility military helicopter used for transport, medevac, and combat support.

The World's Largest Cargo Plane

Antonov An-225 Mriya

First flown in 1988, the world's largest cargo aircraft, designed to transport oversized loads.
